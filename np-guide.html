Typical Impeller Types

Rushton (Radial)

High shear, gas dispersion, strong radial flow. Common in fermentation.

Typical Np: 4 - 6

Pitched Blade (45 deg)

Axial/radial blend; good for blending and suspending solids.

Typical Np: 1 - 2

Retreat Curve (Retreat Blade)

Gentler shear, often used in glass-lined vessels for dispersions.

Unbaffled Np: 0.30 - 0.35 Partially baffled Np: 0.50 - 0.70 Fully baffled Np: 1.06 - 1.10

Hydrofoil (Axial)

Efficient axial flow, lower shear; often used for bulk blending.

Typical Np: 0.2 - 0.8

How to Choose Np and Deck Count

Factor Impact Rule of thumb
Impeller type Sets baseline Np range Pick Np from vendor charts or validated literature.
Flow regime Np can vary in laminar or transitional flow Use turbulent Np only when Re is high enough.
Impeller decks Total power scales with number of impellers P_total ~ n_imp * Np * rho * N^3 * D^5.
Diameter ratio Changes torque and tip speed Keep D/T consistent if possible during scale-up.
Use vendor curves when available. This guide is indicative and should be validated for your specific geometry and baffle setup.
